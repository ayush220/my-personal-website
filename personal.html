The Paradox of Choice

In today's world, we're constantly bombarded with choices - from the mundane (which brand of toothpaste to buy) to the significant (which career path to pursue). While having options is generally seen as positive, there's a point where too many choices can lead to decision fatigue and decreased satisfaction.

I've found that implementing constraints in my own life - whether it's limiting my wardrobe choices or setting boundaries around work hours - has paradoxically led to greater freedom and happiness. Sometimes, less really is more.

— Ayush, May 2025

×

Digital Minimalism

In an age where our attention is constantly pulled in a dozen different directions by notifications, emails, and social media, I've been experimenting with digital minimalism. It's not about rejecting technology outright, but about being more intentional with how we use it.

Some practices I've found helpful: scheduling specific times to check email, using website blockers during focused work sessions, and keeping my phone out of the bedroom. The goal isn't to be disconnected, but to ensure that technology serves us rather than the other way around.

— Ayush, April 2025
